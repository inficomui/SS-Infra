# Global Health Overview: Pediatric Palliative Care

## Introduction
Pediatric palliative care (PPC) is a specialized medical field focused on improving the quality of life for children with life-limiting or life-threatening conditions and their families. It addresses physical, psychological, social, and spiritual needs throughout the course of an illness.

## Global Need and Access
- **Annual Need**: Approximately 21 million children worldwide require some form of palliative care each year.
- **Access Gap**: It is estimated that less than 1% of children globally who need palliative care actually receive it.
- **Geographic Disparity**: Over 95% of children needing PPC live in low- and middle-income countries, where resources and specialized training are often most lacking.

## Key Principles of Pediatric Palliative Care
- **Holistic Care**: Includes symptom management, psychological support, social services, and spiritual care.
- **Family-Centered**: Focuses on the entire family unit, providing support for siblings and parents/guardians.
- **Multi-disciplinary Teams**: Involves a range of professionals, including doctors, nurses, social workers, psychologists, and chaplains.
- **Age-Appropriate**: Tailored to the developmental stage and unique needs of children at different ages.

## Top 10 Global Facts
1. **High Volume of Need**: 21 million children need palliative care annually.
2. **Limited Reach**: Only a fraction of children in need have access to these services.
3. **Primary Drivers**: Leading causes include congenital anomalies, neonatal conditions, and NCDs.
4. **Economic Inequality**: Most children in need are in low-income regions.
5. **Quality of Life Focus**: The goal is comfort and quality over curative treatment when cure is no longer possible.
6. **Unique Pediatric Requirements**: Children’s palliative care is distinct from adult care due to developmental factors.
7. **Emotional and Spiritual Needs**: Essential components often provided by psychologists and spiritual leaders.
8. **Home vs. Hospital**: A growing trend towards providing care in the home environment.
9. **Impact on Families**: PPC significantly reduces the emotional and financial burden on families.
10. **International Advocacy**: The ICPCN and WHO are leading efforts to integrate PPC into national health systems.
